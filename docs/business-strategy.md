# FinFlow Business Strategy — WordwideFX Pivot

**Date:** 2026-03-25
**Status:** Strategy defined, presentation ready

---

## 1. The Pivot

WordwideFX has been providing financial translation services for 15+ years. The traditional translation business is declining because clients are using ChatGPT and generic AI for translations — with no quality controls, no compliance awareness, and no terminology consistency.

**Strategy:** Pivot from "human translation provider" to "AI-powered financial content platform" — leveraging our 15 years of client-specific data as the competitive moat.

---

## 2. Core Value Proposition

1. **We already know your language** — client-specific glossaries, tone profiles, compliance terminology built from years of actual translations
2. **AI + Human expertise** — not replacing translators, augmenting them with learning AI that gets better every month
3. **End-to-end pipeline** — from news monitoring to published, compliance-approved, multi-language reports in 30 minutes
4. **Agent deliberation** — TA and FA agents debate like a real research desk until consensus
5. **Compliance built-in** — MiFID II, SEC/FINRA, FCA, ASIC, MAS — not bolted on after the fact

---

## 3. Competitive Moat (What No One Can Replicate)

- **100+ financial clients served over 15 years** — we have their communication style, terminology, and tone in our DNA
- **40+ languages with financial specialization** — not generic translation, financial-specific
- **Client-specific glossaries** — "margin call," "spread," "pip" translated correctly per broker and per jurisdiction
- **Existing relationships** — we can approach clients we already work with (or have worked with) with a warm intro
- **Translation learning loop** — human corrections feed back into AI, creating an ever-improving system that locks clients in

---

## 4. Client Retention Strategy (The Learning Curve)

The adaptive HITL model creates natural long-term engagement:

| Period | HITL % | Automation % | Client Perception |
|--------|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| Month 1-2 | 100% | 0% | "Building our profile" |
| Month 3-4 | 70% | 30% | "Getting better" |
| Month 5-6 | 50% | 50% | "Impressive quality" |
| Month 7-8 | 20% | 80% | "Almost autonomous" |
| Month 9-12 | 10% | 90% | "Spot-check only" |

**Key insight:** Clients stay because:
1. The system gets measurably better each month (they can see it)
2. Switching to a competitor means starting from zero (no glossary, no tone, no learning)
3. The cost decreases over time as automation increases
4. They've invested in training the system — sunk cost effect

## 8. What We're NOT Doing

- Not competing on price with generic AI translation (race to the bottom)
- Not building a general-purpose translation tool (staying financial-only)
- Not removing humans entirely (HITL is a feature, not a bug — clients trust it)
- Not trying to replace Bloomberg (we augment, not replace, data sources)
