The Solution

13 metrics. Objective scoring.
Pass or fail.

The FinFlow Translation Engine decomposes translation quality into 13 discrete, measurable metrics, each scored from 0 to 100. Every metric has a minimum threshold, and every threshold is configurable per client, per language. A document passes when every metric meets its threshold and the aggregate score clears the overall bar.

This transforms translation quality from a subjective judgment call into a verifiable, repeatable process. The same document, scored against the same profile, produces the same result every time — regardless of who submitted it or when.

The question is no longer "Is this a good translation?" It becomes: "Does this translation meet OANDA's quality standard for Spanish (Spain) institutional content?" And the answer is a number.

Quality Pipeline

The multi-layer correction architecture

When a translation does not meet its quality thresholds, the system does not simply retry. Retrying with the same approach produces the same result. Instead, the FinFlow engine routes failed metrics to domain-specific correction specialists — each one focused exclusively on its area of expertise.

Corrections compound sequentially: each specialist builds on the output of the one before it, and each is explicitly instructed to preserve corrections outside its domain. A Quality Arbiter orchestrates the sequence, detects conflicts, and decides when to escalate to human review.

Translation Quality Pipeline

Maximum two correction rounds per document. If thresholds still are not met, the system escalates to human review with a complete audit trail — every correction attempted, every score change, every specialist's reasoning. The reviewer sees exactly what was tried and where the system reached its limit.

How profiles are built

Onboarding a new client requires no manual configuration. Provide pairs of original documents alongside their approved translations, and the engine analyzes the differences to extract every dimension of the profile automatically — glossary, tone, regional variant, brand rules, and quality baselines.

The system then validates itself by scoring the client's own approved translations against the extracted profile. If the client's own work scores above 90, the profile is reliable. If not, the extraction is refined.

Regional Intelligence

Not just the language.
The variant.

Financial institutions operate across markets with distinct linguistic conventions. A compliance document for Madrid and a market report for Buenos Aires require different Spanish — not just different vocabulary, but different grammar, spelling conventions, and reader expectations. The FinFlow engine tracks and enforces these distinctions at the metric level.

Variant Vocabulary Example Grammar Marker Usage
Spanish (Spain) "ordenador" / "vosotros" Vosotros conjugation, leismo European institutional clients
Spanish (Argentina) "computadora" / "vos" Voseo, distinct verb forms Southern Cone markets
Spanish (Mexico) "computadora" / "ustedes" Ustedes standard, distinct idioms North American Spanish markets
Spanish (Colombia) "computador" / "ustedes" Ustedeo, regional vocabulary Andean market coverage
English (British) "authorised" / "colour" -ise spellings, collective noun agreement UK/EU regulatory filings

The Regional Variant Correctness metric detects variant markers in every translation and flags inconsistencies — a single "vosotros" in a Latin American document, or a British spelling in an American report. These are not style preferences. In regulated financial communications, they are errors.

The system learns from every correction

Every human edit — whether from a reviewer, a client, or a compliance officer — feeds back into the client profile. The engine analyzes what changed, why it changed, and updates the glossary, tone model, and quality baselines accordingly.

This creates a compounding advantage: the more documents you translate, the fewer corrections you need. Early engagements may require human review on most documents. Within months, the system converges on client preferences and review rates drop to a fraction of their initial level.
